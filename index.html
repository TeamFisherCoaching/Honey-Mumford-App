Discover Your Honey & Mumford Learning Style

The Honey & Mumford model identifies four distinct learning preferences — Activist, Reflector, Theorist and Pragmatist. Understanding your mix helps you learn faster, coach more effectively and tailor development journeys that actually stick.

At‑a‑glance overview of the styles

ActivistReflectorTheoristPragmatist
  • Jump in feet‑first.
  • Love novelty & variety.
  • Prefer group activities.
  • Observe before acting.
  • Collect & analyse data.
  • Enjoy reflective journals.
  • Seek rational models.
  • Value logic & structure.
  • Ask “why does it work?”
  • Focus on application.
  • Experiment with ideas.
  • Value quick results.

Why it matters in coaching

  • Self‑reflection for coachees: Spot blind‑spots and play to strengths when tackling new challenges.
  • Coaching agility: Adapt questions, feedback and practice tasks to each individual’s dominant style.
  • Balanced development: Surface the less‑used quadrants so learners grow in all modes.
